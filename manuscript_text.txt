1. Introduction

1.1. The Organization



The name of the organization chosen for this study is Cecille's N' Style

Boutique, a small retail clothing store located in Davao City. The representative and

owner of the business is Mrs. Cecille B. Ilanga, who oversees daily operations including

sales, employee management, and supplier coordination. The boutique specializes in

trendy apparel and accessories for men and women, catering primarily to walk-in

customers within the local area.

1.2. Organization Brief Description



Cecille's N' Style Boutique operates within the retail fashion industry,

focusing on clothing and fashion accessories. The store caters to both walk-in

customers and occasional bulk orders from loyal clients. The organizational structure is

simple -- with the Store Owner/Manager at the top, followed by a small staff of sales

clerks who manage customer orders and handle transactions. The store manager is

responsible for supervising operations, managing inventory, monitoring employee

attendance, and overseeing financial reports. The sales clerks, on the other hand, assist

customers with product inquiries, record sales transactions, and update order

information. The boutique started as a small clothing stall and has since expanded into

a full boutique, although operations remain primarily manual.

1.3. Current Business Process
At present, Cecille's N' Style Boutique operates with a semi-manual workflow that relies heavily on paper records and handwritten documentation. The following summarizes their current processes:
 Sales and Ordering (Checkout) - Customers select items for purchase with assistance from sales clerks. The sales clerks manually compute prices and write down receipts. Because there is no barcode scanning system, checking out multiple items is slow, tedious, and prone to human error during manual price calculations.
 Inventory and Bale Management - Stock monitoring is done by visually checking physical stock levels. The boutique frequently purchases clothing in bulk (bales) from suppliers. Currently, there is no systematic way to track the source of these bales or accurately break them down into individual, sellable items. Replenishment orders are made only when the owner notices shortages, and there is no formal system to track supplier records, stock-ins, or damaged goods.
 Employee Access and Security - Employee details are kept on paper. Currently, the store operates without strict system access controls, meaning any employee handling the cash register has the same level of access as the owner, which poses a security risk for business data.

3

 Supplier and Bale Purchasing - The boutique purchases clothing stocks from suppliers, often in bale form. These bale purchases are manually recorded, and details such as supplier name, purchase date, bale category, cost, quantity, and received items are not consistently tracked in one centralized record. After purchasing, the bale contents are sorted into premium, standard, and damaged items. However, the breakdown of each bale is still manually monitored, making it difficult to trace which products came from a specific bale batch.

1.4. Identified Problems





Based on observation and interviews with the store owner, several operational

problems were identified:

1. Lack of a centralized record system - Employee, customer, and product records are kept manually, leading to inconsistencies and delays in retrieval.
2. Unmonitored inventory movement - Stock-ins and stock-outs are not systematically tracked, leading to inaccurate inventory counts.
3. Inefficient bulk (bale) inventory breakdown - Items purchased in bulk bales from suppliers are difficult to track individually, leading to pricing and stock inaccuracies.
4. Slow and manual checkout processes - The absence of a barcode scanner system for processing multiple items at once slows down sales operations.
5. Lack of strict user access control - Roles and permissions are not strictly defined, risking unauthorized access to sensitive financial and inventory data.
6. Untracked bale purchase and breakdown records Ł Bale purchases, supplier orders, received quantities, returned bales, and bale breakdown details are not centrally monitored, making it difficult to know the exact category, cost, quantity, and inventory movement of each bale batch.

2. Proposed System The proposed system, Cecille's N' Style Boutique Management System, is designed to automate and streamline key operations within the boutique. The system focuses on Inventory Management (with specialized Bale Purchasing and Breakdown features), a Point of Sale (POS) system integrated with Barcode Scanning, Supplier Management, and an Advanced Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) module. Each subsystem handles specific organizational functions but remains securely interconnected through a shared, automated database.

The manager inputs the total pieces found inside a bulk bale. They sort the items into Class A (Premium) and Class B (Standard). If defective items are found, they are recorded. The system then automatically converts these into individual stock records.

System: (1.1) Loads bale data. (2.1) Validates piece counts against total. (3.1) Generates stock-in records.
